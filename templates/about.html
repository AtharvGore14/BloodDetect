About Our Technology

Fingerprints are unique to each individual, formed by ridges and valleys on the skin's surface. Even identical twins have distinct fingerprints. This uniqueness arises from both genetic and environmental factors during fetal development, making fingerprints a reliable biometric identifier.

How It Works

Blood group antigens, like A, B, AB, and O, are determined by specific inherited alleles. These genes may be linked to physical traits that influence fingerprint patterns, allowing machine learning models to make blood group predictions based on them.

Practical Applications

In forensics, predicting blood groups from fingerprints can provide crucial leads. In medical emergencies where a patient's blood type is unknown, this approach enables instant identification without invasive tests.
